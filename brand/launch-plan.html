Instagram Launch Plan

Launch it right, without burning out.

A realistic, week-by-week plan to launch and grow an authentic OB-GYN + mom + NHSC-scholar account — built around a busy clinical life.

Positioning

Say this in one breath

Board-certified OB-GYN, boy-mom of two, and NHSC scholar sharing the real story of medicine, motherhood, and how she got here — the honest, funny, and useful version.

  • Niche: women's health + the doctor-mom life + "the path here."
  • Promise: "I'll answer what you're too afraid to Google."
  • Voice: warm, funny, self-deprecating, credible.
  • Edge: a real board-certified physician + the NHSC story almost nobody explains.

Cadence

This is the whole game

Consistency beats volume. Never disappear for a week — the algorithm punishes gaps harder than a lighter cadence.

3 posts / week · Mon · Wed · Fri

Batch-film once a week (record 4–6 clips in one 60–90 min session), schedule the whole week in advance, and keep Stories going 2–4×/week. If 3/week ever feels heavy, drop to 2 before you drop quality.

Every post fits one of four

Rotate these and keep a running idea bank so batch days are never "what do I film?"

Phase 1 · Days 4–7

Batch your first week

  • Film the first 4 Reels in one session (Week 1 scripts below). Change tops between clips so they look filmed on different days.
  • Edit + caption (CapCut/Submagic): strong hook in first 1–2s, captions on, custom cover.
  • Schedule Mon/Wed/Fri via Meta Business Suite (free).
  • Post 3–5 Reels before announcing — so new visitors land on a real feed, not an empty one.

Growth mechanics

Do these, skip the gimmicks

  • Hook in the first 1–2 seconds (on-screen + spoken). This is 80% of performance.
  • Captions always on — most viewers are muted.
  • Search-optimize: real keywords in audio, on-screen text, and caption.
  • 3–5 specific hashtags (#obgyn #womenshealth #medmom #nhsc), not 30.
  • Engage the first 30–60 min after posting — reply to every comment.
  • Reply to comments as Reels — free content that rewards commenters.
  • Consistency > perfection.
  • Best times (2026 data): Wed ~12pm/6pm — but trust her own Insights after 2–3 weeks.

Non-negotiable

Medical-creator guardrails

Protect the license & the trust

  • Never film or reference identifiable patients / PHI — no charts, screens, or faces in the background.
  • Add "educational, not medical advice — talk to your own doctor" on anything clinical.
  • Stay in her lane and cite guidelines (ACOG etc.) where relevant.
  • Check her employer's social-media policy; don't imply institutional endorsement.
  • Don't over-automate the voice — authenticity is the entire moat for a physician.

What "working" looks like

Metrics, in order of importance

  1. Follower growth trend (not the absolute number early on).
  2. Saves + shares — the signals that actually drive reach.
  3. Watch-through / retention — are people watching past the hook?
  4. Comments — community = durability.
  5. Likes are the least important. Don't optimize for them.

Review monthly: which pillar and hook style performed best → make more of that.
