Your caption style, step by step

This is the look for your Reel captions — bold, easy to read on mute, with the word you're saying lit up in gold. Set it up once in CapCut, then reuse it on every video. Here's exactly how.

What it should look like

The gold highlight moves to the word you're saying, one at a time.

The exact look

Caption text#EAF0F7 · cream
Highlighted word — background#D4A537 · gold
Highlighted word — text#0B2545 · navy

Font: Mulish, Bold / ExtraBold. On phone, if Mulish isn't there, pick the closest heavy sans. Sentence case — not ALL CAPS.

Set it up in CapCut (you have Pro)

  1. Auto-caption your clip. Bottom bar → Captions → Auto captions → Create. It writes captions from your voice.
  2. Fix any wrong words. Tap Edit and correct medical terms it mishears.
  3. Set the font. On your laptop (CapCut Pro) you can import the real Mulish font: Text → Fonts → Import. On your phone, pick the closest heavy / ExtraBold sans.
  4. Color + shadow. Set text to cream (#EAF0F7) and turn on a soft Shadow (low opacity) so it's readable over any background. Use Apply to all so you only style once.
  5. The gold highlight. Browse the Pro caption / text templates + Animations for a word-by-word "highlight" style, then recolor it: highlight gold (#D4A537), highlighted-word text navy (#0B2545).
    Check one thing: does the highlight style let you set a custom color? Some do, some lock it. If it won't turn gold, use the fallback: highlight just the 1–2 key words in each caption — put that word on its own text layer with a gold background + navy text. Don't gold every word.
  6. Position. Drag the captions to the lower third, centered — above where Instagram's buttons and caption sit.
  7. Reuse it next time. Keep one template project with the styled captions and duplicate it for each new Reel, or copy a styled caption and paste it onto the next clip.

Keep in mind

  • Captions always on — most people watch on mute.
  • Make the first 1–2 seconds (your hook) captioned and punchy.
  • Big text, few words per line — it reads better on a phone than cramming a full sentence.
  • On anything clinical, add "educational, not medical advice" in the caption.
